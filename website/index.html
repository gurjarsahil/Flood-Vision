About Flood Vision

Our mission is to reduce the impact of flooding through advanced prediction and early warning systems.

The Challenge

Floods are among the most devastating natural disasters, affecting millions of people worldwide each year. Traditional flood prediction methods often fall short in accuracy and timeliness.

Our Solution

Flood Vision combines satellite imagery, weather data, and topographical information with advanced machine learning algorithms to predict flood patterns with unprecedented accuracy.

Our Technology

Cutting-edge technology for accurate flood prediction and monitoring

Satellite Imagery

We analyze multi-spectral satellite data from sources like Sentinel-1, Sentinel-2, and MODIS to detect water bodies and changes over time.

Machine Learning

Our deep learning models, including U-Net and ResNet architectures, process imagery to identify flood-prone areas with high precision.

Weather Integration

Real-time weather data integration helps predict rainfall patterns and potential flooding events up to 72 hours in advance.

Terrain Analysis

Digital elevation models and terrain analysis identify natural water flow paths and flood-prone depressions.

Big Data Processing

Our platform processes terabytes of data daily to provide up-to-date flood predictions and risk assessments.

GIS Integration

Geographic Information Systems allow visualization of flood predictions on interactive maps for easy interpretation.

Computer Vision Analysis

Our flood analysis system uses advanced computer vision and deep learning to analyze satellite imagery:

Image Segmentation

Using U-Net architecture to identify water bodies, land, vegetation, and infrastructure with pixel-level precision.

Water Detection

Spectral analysis to differentiate between permanent water bodies and recent flooding using NIR band reflection patterns.

Topographical Analysis

Integration of digital elevation models to predict water flow and identify natural channels and depressions.

Risk Assessment

CNN-based classification models evaluate flood impact based on infrastructure proximity and terrain characteristics.
